Artist Spotlight: Maya Chen

By GetMinted · March 23, 2026 · Austin, TX

Maya Chen has built a reputation in Austin as the artist to call for precise, intentional work — geometric mandalas, fine line botanicals, and sacred geometry pieces that look as clean in person as they do on Instagram. With 15K followers and a waiting list that regularly runs eight weeks out, she's one of the most in-demand independent artists in Central Texas.

We sat down with Maya to talk about her path into tattooing, how she built her following, and what changed when she stopped relying entirely on Instagram to run her business.

How did you get into geometric and fine line work specifically?

"Honestly, it started with an obsession with math I didn't expect to have," Maya laughs. "I studied graphic design in college, and the geometric side of design — sacred geometry, tessellations, the way shapes nest inside each other — that just translated directly to skin. My first few years I was doing everything, but I kept gravitating back to the precise stuff. Eventually I just leaned into it fully."

The pivot paid off. Once Maya stopped trying to be everything to every client and positioned herself as the geometric/fine line specialist in Austin, her bookings changed in character. "The clients I get now come in with real ideas, real references. They want this specific thing. That makes every session better."

You've grown to 15K followers without any paid promotion. What's worked?

"Consistency, mostly. I post healed photos alongside fresh photos — which a lot of artists skip — and I think that makes a huge difference. Clients can see what my work actually looks like six months later. That builds trust you can't get from a fresh piece."

Maya also credits Reels with accelerating her growth. "I started filming 20-second time-lapses of my line work about two years ago. Nothing fancy — my phone on a mount above the arm. Those clips routinely get 10x the reach of my static photos. Instagram pushes video hard and I just took advantage of that."

"The clients who find me on Instagram and then go to my website are 10x easier to work with. They've already read how I work, what I charge, and what I need from them. The consultation is half as long."

Tell us about the shift to having your own website.

"Before, everything was Instagram DMs. Someone would see my work, slide into my DMs, we'd go back and forth for a week about sizing and placement and deposit, and half the time they'd ghost me anyway. I was losing so much time."

Maya launched her GetMinted website in early 2025. The difference, she says, was immediate. "I put my intake form on the site — style, size, placement, references, budget — and I require a deposit to confirm the date. Now when someone books, they come in prepared. The flaky inquiries basically disappeared because they won't bother filling out a form."

She also noticed something she didn't expect: better Google traffic. "I started getting inquiries from people who found me on Google searching for geometric tattoo artists in Austin. I never had that before. Those clients are serious — they searched, they found me, they booked. No Instagram required."

What advice would you give an artist just starting to build their presence?

"Niche down faster than you think you need to. I wasted two years trying to appeal to everyone. The moment I committed fully to geometric and fine line, everything got easier — better clients, better referrals, cleaner feed, clearer identity."

"And get a website early. Not a fancy one — just something that has your work, your process, and a way to book. Instagram is rented land. Your website is yours."
