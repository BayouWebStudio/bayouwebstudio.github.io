Artist Spotlight: Carlos Reyes

By GetMinted · April 13, 2026 · Houston, TX

Carlos Reyes is the kind of tattoo artist people drive across Texas to see. Based out of Houston's East End, he specializes in black and grey realism — portraits, religious imagery, and large-scale pieces that look more like photographs than tattoos. With around 8K Instagram followers and a client roster built almost entirely on word-of-mouth, Carlos represents the quiet grind that makes Houston's tattoo scene one of the strongest in the country.

We caught up with Carlos between sessions to talk about his journey, how he built his business, and what having his own website has done for his bookings.

What got you into black and grey realism?

"I grew up drawing. Always. My abuela had these old photos of family members in Mexico — black and white, faded, beautiful. I'd try to copy them with a pencil. That's literally what I do now, except on skin."

Carlos apprenticed at a shop in Montrose when he was 20 and spent his first three years doing everything: traditional, lettering, small pieces. "But any time someone wanted a portrait, I'd volunteer. The other guys in the shop didn't want the pressure. I loved it. Every portrait is a puzzle — getting the likeness right, the shading, the depth. I just stayed with it."

You've built your business mostly through referrals. How?

"Houston has a strong tattoo community, and in this city, word of mouth is king. If someone's mom sees a portrait tattoo of their grandmother and it actually looks like her? That person tells five people. I've gotten entire families as clients because one person came in and the piece turned out right."

Carlos doesn't run ads and doesn't do collaborations for reach. "My marketing is doing the best work I can on every single piece. That sounds like a non-answer, but it's the truth. When someone shows off a tattoo and people say 'who did that?' — that's a referral that's already sold."

"I used to lose probably two or three bookings a week because people would DM me, I'd be mid-session, and by the time I responded hours later they'd already booked someone else. That's real money walking away."

What made you decide to get a website?

"Honestly? Losing bookings. I was doing everything through DMs and it was chaos. Someone would send me a reference, I'd ask about size and placement, they'd take a day to respond, I'd forget to follow up — and the booking never happens. Multiply that by a few times a week and it adds up fast."

Carlos set up his GetMinted site in late 2025. "The biggest thing was the inquiry form. Now someone fills out exactly what they want — references, size, placement, timeline — and I can look at it between sessions and just say yes or no. No back-and-forth. No forgotten conversations."

"The other thing I didn't expect: Google traffic. People search for 'realistic tattoo artist Houston' and I come up. These are people who don't follow me on Instagram — they're brand new leads. That never happened before I had a website."

How has your booking process changed since getting the site?

"Night and day. Before, I was managing bookings in my Notes app and my DMs. Now I have a proper form, people submit deposits, and I know who's coming in next week without checking three different apps. My no-show rate dropped to basically zero once I started requiring deposits through the site."

"The clients are better prepared too. When someone fills out a form with references and a size estimate, they've already thought it through. The consultation is shorter, the session goes smoother, and the result is better. Everyone wins."

What would you tell a tattoo artist who's still running everything through Instagram?

"You're leaving money on the table. I know because I did it for years. Instagram is great for showing your work, but it's terrible for running a business. You need somewhere to send people that handles the business part — the booking, the deposit, the info you need before someone sits in your chair."

"And it doesn't have to be complicated. My site is clean, it shows my work, it has a form, and it tells people where I am. That's all it needs to do. Get the website, put the link in your bio, and watch what happens."
